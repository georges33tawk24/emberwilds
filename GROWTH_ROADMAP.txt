EMBERWILDS — GROWTH & ENGAGEMENT ROADMAP
Research-backed evaluation of the 24-part "growth ecosystem" proposal,
scored on impact-vs-effort for a SOLO dev building a HAND-CRAFTED NARRATIVE
platformer. Read this before building any retention/growth feature.
Produced from a 3-angle web-research pass (retention/virality, SEO/discovery,
solo-dev live-service reality) + a scored matrix + synthesis.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE VERDICT (read this if nothing else)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The 24-part prompt is a GOOD research dump and a BAD roadmap. Its DNA is
live-service giants (Fortnite, Clash Royale, Geometry Dash), and ~1/3 of it is
machinery for games EMBERWILDS is NOT. The prompt's instinct to INSTRUMENT, add
LEADERBOARDS, and build a SHAREABLE RESULT is exactly right. Its instinct to add
ACCOUNTS, a LEVEL EDITOR, SEASONAL DROPS, and a REFERRAL ENGINE is a trap that
turns a solo designer into a full-time moderation-and-support desk for an
audience that doesn't exist yet.

EMBERWILDS' two real enemies are (1) the retention CLIFF at "the end" of a
linear campaign, and (2) OBSCURITY. Not a thin content pipeline that needs
perpetual feeding. So: build the cheap, self-moderating, campaign-reinforcing
wins now; hard-cut the treadmill; stay on a static host as long as possible.

THE UNFAIR ADVANTAGE: the sim is deterministic + Phaser-free, so a server can
re-simulate a submitted input recording. Cheat-proof leaderboards + ghost
replays — normally a multi-week slog — are uniquely cheap here. That's the one
piece of live-service tech worth building, and few solo devs can afford it.

BIGGEST STRATEGIC INSIGHT FROM RESEARCH (not in the prompt):
For a solo browser-game dev, the #1 discovery channel is NOT your own SEO — it's
AGGREGATOR PORTALS (CrazyGames ~104M MAU, Poki ~152M) that algorithmically PUSH
your game for free. But they gate visibility on hard requirements: a
leaderboard, a share button, an ad SDK, a save system, and short-session-
friendly onboarding. So the Phase-1 features below aren't just retention — they
are the ADMISSION TICKET to the channel that actually brings players. Build
them, then submit to CrazyGames (take the ~2-month exclusivity for +50% rev
share), then Poki, then a distributor (GameDistribution) to blanket small sites.

THE 4-PHASE PLAN (concrete)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 1 — "MAKE IT STICKY & DISCOVERABLE" (mostly no backend)
  * Achievements: ~30 feat-gated (no-damage clear, all-tokens world, stomp chains,
    speed thresholds, secret finds). Client-side, in the versioned save.
  * Cosmetic scarves tied to the hardest achievements (visible progression, $0).
  * Player stats: deaths, jumps, distance, perfect runs, per-level best — from the
    sim, shown on a stats screen + the clear tally (death-positive framing).
  * Exploration depth: at least one real secret room per level (mostly done in
    Phase 2A), lore drops, and an optional secret boss down the line.
  * World-map polish: animate region unlocks, show completion %, boss markers.
  * Analytics: a lightweight privacy-friendly beacon (or Cloudflare Web Analytics)
    for D1/D7, session length, and per-level quit points. Instrument BEFORE content.
  * SEO: DONE (JSON-LD, OG card, sitemap, crawlable content).
  * Performance: hold 60fps on mid mobile; code-split the 1.6MB bundle.
  * THEN: submit to CrazyGames (take exclusivity), Poki, a distributor; list on
    itch.io/Newgrounds timed with a game jam; post authentically to r/WebGames.

PHASE 2 — "COMPETE & SHARE" (first backend + the unique asset)
  * Replay recording: capture the input stream per run (the sim is deterministic).
  * Ghost racing: race your PB, the WR, or a friend's ghost from a share link.
  * Leaderboards: global + per-level speedrun + auto-resetting weekly, with
    server-side replay validation (re-simulate the input stream in a Worker).
  * Share cards: "I beat LEVEL in TIME with N deaths" image + one-tap copy.
  * Game modes: time-attack, boss-rush, hardcore/permadeath (reuse levels).
  * Static changelog/roadmap page; weekly storytelling patch notes.

PHASE 3 — "DEPTH AT SCALE" (once traffic justifies the maintenance)
  * Daily challenge: seeded remixes of existing levels + its own board + streak.
  * Small cosmetic set expansion (still achievement-gated, no purchases).
  * Auto-generated wiki/guide pages for long-tail SEO.
  * Media/clip auto-capture from replays for shareable highlights.

PHASE 4 — "FUTURE VISION" (only if the game is already successful)
  * Accounts (OAuth only, never roll your own) IF a social loop demands it.
  * Referral cosmetics, opt-in notifications. Everything cosmetic, never P2W.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BACKEND DECISION
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Stay STATIC as long as humanly possible. Everything in Phase 1 except nothing
(all of Phase 1 is client-side) ships on Cloudflare Pages with no server.

When leaderboards force your hand (Phase 2): Cloudflare Workers + D1 (SQLite,
ORDER BY/LIMIT free) + KV caching the hot top-N. Scope the ENTIRE first backend
to ONE Worker with TWO routes:
  POST /score       -> Cloudflare Turnstile check + server-side sanity-cap +
                       per-device rate-limit + insert; THEN layer replay
                       re-simulation validation (the gold standard you can afford).
  GET  /leaderboard -> cached top-N from KV.
Identity = a random device UUID in localStorage + a profanity-filtered display
name. NO accounts, NO moderation queue, NO GDPR deletion flow. $0 on the free
tier (D1 5GB / 5M reads-day, Workers 100K req-day). Add nothing heavier until
real player demand — not the prompt — justifies the ongoing cost.

KEY RESEARCH TAKEAWAYS (the principles behind the plan)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RETENTION / VIRALITY
  * Campaign games retain via MASTERY + hidden collectibles + optional challenges
    + death-positive framing — but they END (a hard cliff). Bolt ENDLESS hooks on
    post-completion: challenge/deathless modes, speedrun variants, collectible
    hunts, a named difficulty ladder.
  * Named, ascending, POSTABLE milestones (Balatro Antes, GD Demon tiers) structure
    the D7/D30 long tail; finishing a hard tier is itself the shareable install-driver.
  * Ship the CLIP-GENERATOR inside the game: engineer setup->payoff "holy-crap"
    moments that read in a 15-second clip (clear framing + reaction cue + visible payoff).
  * Publish a permissive creator policy ("streamers can monetize") + personally seed
    the game to mid-size streamers. Free marketing (worked for Balatro, Slither.io).
  * Retention benchmarks are brutal (median D7 ~3.4%). Track quit points, ship small
    VISIBLE weekly patches with storytelling notes (VS's #1 long-term driver).
  * Obsess over the FIRST 10 MINUTES — a strong self-contained slice converts players
    into advocates; that commitment beats vanity top-of-funnel numbers.

SEO / DISCOVERY
  * Portals (CrazyGames/Poki) > your own SEO. Build their requirements IN, submit,
    take CrazyGames exclusivity for +50% rev share.
  * itch.io is a listing, not a traffic source; time a launch with a GAME JAM (highest-
    ROI free discovery), fill every tag, post devlogs for "freshness".
  * Reddit (r/WebGames) > Discord for COLD discovery (74% of gaming Redditors aren't on
    Discord). Discord is a RETENTION tool — stand one up only once you have players.
  * VideoGame JSON-LD is cheap insurance for rich snippets + AI-answer surfacing, not a
    growth engine. Long-tail wiki SEO compounds but pays off months out.
  * WASTE OF TIME pre-audience: paid ads, an elaborate marketing site expecting Google
    traffic, head-term SEO ("free games"), spreading thin across 15 tiny portals.

SOLO-DEV / LIVE-SERVICE REALITY
  * Tier features by MAINTENANCE COST. Cheap+safe: leaderboards, seeded dailies, stats,
    achievements. Expensive+dangerous: accounts, UGC/level editors, chat, real-time MP,
    prize competitions.
  * UGC inherits the full internet moderation problem with no solo-scalable answer.
  * Accounts = hidden time-sink + legal liability (GDPR erasure/export, breach). Use
    device UUIDs or OAuth; never store passwords; a leaderboard needs only name + token.
  * Client-submitted scores are faked within days. Minimum anti-cheat: Turnstile +
    server sanity-cap + rate-limit (80% of value, ~1 day). True anti-cheat = replay
    re-simulation (a real project — do it only for competitive boards, which you can).
  * Prefer STATELESS, SELF-EXPIRING, SELF-MODERATING designs: auto-resetting boards,
    profanity-filtered names, no free-text surfaces between users.

===============================================================================
BOTTOM LINE: Build leaderboards+ghosts (your unfair advantage), achievements,
stats, exploration, world-map polish, analytics, and the portal-readiness
checklist. Submit to CrazyGames. HARD-CUT the level editor, accounts, seasonal
events, and referral engine. Stay static until the leaderboard needs one Worker.
Grow through portals + Reddit + streamer seeding, not through building Fortnite.
